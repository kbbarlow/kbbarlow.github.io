Self-guided Tours of Cozumel

About the Island

Cozumel is a jungle island in the Caribbean Sea, about 10 miles off the eastern coast of the Yucatán Peninsula, Mexico. Clear, warm water, spectacular coral reefs, and abundant marine life make Cozumel one of the world’s best scuba-diving destinations, as well as a major resort.

During the Mayan period Cozumel was a commercial and ceremonial center, and today the island has several small archaeological sites. In the early 1500’s, the Spanish led expeditions to Cozumel where the island was used as a base for efforts to conquer the Maya. Cozumel was an ideal center of operations because of the abundant offshore reefs which provided protection.

There are many activities to enjoy on Cozumel. From scuba diving, off-roading, and exploring ancient ruins, to fine-dining, luxury accomodations, and miles of white sandy beaches. Cozumel is a tropical paradise that’s more relaxed than any other Caribbean location.

Top-rated Experiences

A Taste of the Ancient

half-day

Imagine yourself living in another time and culture as you embark on a 30 minute scooter ride to the ruins of San Gervasio. Explore the various structures amidst an iguana-filled wildlife refuge. Then stop by Villa Maya for an unforgettable jungle walk and sweat lodge experience. Finally, ride down to the laid-back, white-sand beaches of Playa Chen Rio for incredible access to swimming and snorkeling.

The Wildside Jungle Adventure

full-day

Begin the day with a relaxing ride along the west coast, stopping anywhere that grabs your attention. Make your way to the quant villiage of Cedral for lunch and enjoy the colorful buildings and sleepy way of life. From there, go south and explore the hundreds of jungle paths, fiding your own adventure along the way. Finally, end up at the Hidden Mayan Trails for some breathtaking and sacred cenote swimming.
